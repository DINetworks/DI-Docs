# Spot Trading Guide

Learn how to trade synthetic assets on DSwap, DI Network's spot trading platform that provides access to global markets through oracle-based pricing.

## Getting Started

### Connect Your Wallet
1. Visit the DSwap interface
2. Click "Connect Wallet" 
3. Select your preferred wallet (MetaMask, WalletConnect, etc.)
4. Approve the connection

### Fund Your Account
Before trading, you need DUSD:
1. **Mint DUSD**: Deposit collateral (ETH, WBTC, USDC) to mint DUSD
2. **Buy DUSD**: Purchase from DEXs like Uniswap
3. **Bridge DUSD**: Transfer from other supported chains

## Making Your First Trade

### Step 1: Select Assets
1. Choose your input asset (what you're selling)
2. Choose your output asset (what you're buying)
3. Available assets include crypto, commodities, equities, and forex

### Step 2: Enter Amount
1. Enter the amount you want to trade
2. The interface shows the estimated output amount
3. Review the exchange rate and fees

### Step 3: Execute Trade
1. Click "Swap" to proceed
2. Approve the transaction in your wallet
3. Wait for confirmation (usually 1-2 blocks)

## Trading Strategies

### Dollar-Cost Averaging (DCA)
Gradually build positions over time:
1. Set a regular schedule (daily/weekly)
2. Trade fixed DUSD amounts
3. Reduce timing risk and volatility impact

### Swing Trading
Trade based on price movements:
1. Monitor asset price trends
2. Buy during dips, sell during peaks
3. Use technical analysis for entry/exit points

### Portfolio Rebalancing
Maintain target asset allocation:
1. Set target percentages for each asset
2. Regularly rebalance to maintain ratios
3. Use DSwap for efficient rebalancing

## Risk Management

### Position Sizing
- Never risk more than 5% of portfolio on single trade
- Start with small positions to learn the platform
- Gradually increase size as you gain experience

### Diversification
- Spread investments across asset categories
- Don't concentrate in single asset or sector
- Consider correlation between assets

### Stop Losses
While DSwap doesn't have built-in stop losses:
1. Set mental stop loss levels
2. Monitor positions regularly
3. Execute manual stops when needed

## Common Issues

### Transaction Failures
**Cause**: Insufficient gas or network congestion
**Solution**: Increase gas limit or wait for lower congestion

### Price Differences
**Cause**: Oracle price updates between quote and execution
**Solution**: Use slippage tolerance or retry transaction

### Asset Not Available
**Cause**: Asset trading paused or not supported
**Solution**: Check asset status or try alternative assets
